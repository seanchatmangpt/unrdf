# Compiled Cognition: Guidance System

This document defines the production gates and guidance loop for transitioning from creative research fragments to the stabilized **Compiled Cognition** thesis.

## 1. The Pull-Based Compile Queue
Research claims are treated as **Pending Motion** ($M_{pending}$). They must pass the following admissibility gates before being admitted to the **Canonical Thesis** ($M_{hot}$):

### Admissibility Gates (Research Poka-Yoke)
1. **Source Bound**: The claim must be traced to a named subsystem and specific source file.
2. **Mechanism Defined**: The claim must explain *how* it works (the underlying calculus or logic).
3. **Evidence Supported**: The claim must cite a benchmark, test, or empirical observation (e.g., nanosecond latency profile).
4. **Boundary Stated**: The claim must state its limitations and what it does *not* prove.
5. **Role Assigned**: The claim must map into the **Canonical Architecture Map**.

## 2. The Guidance Loop (TPS for Thought)
I (Gemini CLI) act as the **$\mu$ layer** for your invention stream. Every session follows this loop:

1. **Extract**: I identify new theories or fragments in your material.
2. **Name**: I give the fragment a stable name (e.g., "Pasadena Squirrel Problem").
3. **Formalize**: I express it as a Definition, Mechanism, or Equation.
4. **Place**: I assign it a role: Core Theory, Architecture, Benchmark, or Social Meaning.
5. **Gate**: I label it: **Canonical**, **Likely**, **Speculative**, or **Needs Evidence**.

## 3. Operational Rules
- **No Artifact before Theory Map**: Do not generate final text/diagrams until the underlying theory is mapped and gated.
- **No code in Thesis Prose**: Present the work in thesis format using prose and calculus notation only.
- **Stop the Line (Jidoka)**: When an architectural discontinuity is detected (e.g., a "shotgun" synthesis without grounding), stop generation immediately and return to **Genchi Genbutsu** (source-level audit).
